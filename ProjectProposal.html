Observation done during late night outing with friend:

Identifying the problem:

Observed problems:
Hard to keep track of who payed for what.
Annoying to run after people for recurring payments.
Don’t always have cash on you.
Chain of people owing money to other people trading debts.
Dispute between people over.

Personas:

David: Regular Payer

He/She has to pay a set amount to the Payee. This person must figure out the financially convenient way to do the payment. This can be through electronic transactions or physical currency. He/She must make sure that the amount due is fair amount and evenly distributed according to the purchase. He/She must make sure that there are no erroneous spending.

Tasks:
Owes a recurring payment.
Must be able to defer payment to someone else (eg: sublet)

Christine: Payee

He/she made the initiative to take care of certain bills such recurring bills. He/she uses their name to open an account as multiple name in one account is not possible. He/she also goes shopping for the group such as food, snacks, drink, and group payment to ease the initial billing process. In certain moments, he/she is financially prepared when others cannot provide monetary value when need it immediately.

Tasks:
Collect money owed.
Keep track of payments received.
Makes the initial payment for others.
Incharge of the order process/payment.

Illustrating a use of case scenario:

Scenario 1:

A roommate is going for a grocery run and calls/asks its roommate what they want him/her to buy for them and for the house. This could be due to multiple factors: he/she is near the grocery store, on his/her way to buy food/snacks, or would like to buy something for himself/herself as such kindly asks the others. The buyer goes bad to the apartment and tries to split the bill according to eat roommates orders and apartment purchases. A more organised person would create multiple bills according to the roommates and apartment purchases. In the other hand, because of inconveniences (very long lineup, tired, other reasons), one massive bill was made for all the purchases. Now it has become more complicated to split the cost evenly as one person may have asked for nothing for himself/herself, but only needs to pay for the apartment purchases.

Scenario 2:

For the summer, one of the roommate is going abroad for school/internship. He/she does not want to pay for both housing due to expenses. As such, he/she comes to a conclusion with his/her roommates to sublet his room. To increase the chance for someone to sublet the room, he/she decides to pay for the internet bill for the next 4 months. This now becomes a logistic problem where the other roommate(s) must pursue multiple people for different recurring bills. The other side of the story could that he is no longer paying for the bills but his/her relative.
